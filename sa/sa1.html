• 雜阿含經 1 The Related Discourses

（一） 無常、苦、空、非我 1-4 (1). Impermanent, Painful, Empty, and Not Self

如是我聞： Thus I have heard: t99.2.1a6 一時，佛住舍衛國、祇樹、給孤獨園。 One time, the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in Jeta’s Grove of Śrāvastī.

爾時，世尊告諸比丘： It was then that the Bhagavān addressed the monks, t99.2.1a7 「當觀色無常。 “You should observe that form is [1] impermanent … [2] painful … [3] empty … [4] not self. 如是觀者則為正觀。 Observing it in this way is right view. 正觀 → 正見 (yinshun) 正觀者則生厭離。 Correctly observing it gives rise to disillusionment. 厭離者喜、貪盡。 When someone is disillusioned, delight and greed end. 喜、貪盡者說心解脫。 When delight and greed end, I say their mind is liberated.

「如是觀受⋯想⋯行⋯識無常。 “Thus observe that feeling … conception … volition … consciousness is [1] impermanent … [2] painful … [3] empty … [4] not self. t99.2.1a9 如是觀者則為正觀。 Observing it in this way is right view. 正觀 → 正見 (yinshun) 正觀者則生厭離。 Correctly observing it gives rise to disillusionment. 厭離者喜、貪盡。 When someone is disillusioned, delight and greed end. 喜、貪盡者說心解脫。 When delight and greed end, I say their mind is liberated.

「如是，比丘，心解脫者，若欲自證，則能自證： “Thus, monks, someone who wants to realize the mind’s liberation for themselves can then realize: t99.2.1a12 『我生已盡，梵行已立，所作已作。 ‘My births have been ended, the religious practice has been established, and the task has been accomplished. 自知不受後有。』」 I myself know that I won’t be subject to a later existence.’”

時，諸比丘聞佛所說歡喜，奉行。 When the monks heard what the Buddha taught, they rejoiced and approved. t99.2.1a15
