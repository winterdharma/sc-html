（三） 無知 6 (3). Not Knowing

如是我聞： Thus I have heard: t99.2.1a28 一時，佛住舍衛國、祇樹、給孤獨園。 One time, the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in Jeta’s Grove of Śrāvastī.

爾時，世尊告諸比丘： It was then that the Bhagavān addressed the monks, t99.2.1a29 「於色不知不明，不斷不離欲，則不能斷苦。 “When one doesn’t know or understand form and doesn’t stop or become free of desire for it, it’s not possible to stop suffering. 如是，受⋯想⋯行⋯識不知不明，不斷不離欲，則不能斷苦。 Thus, when one doesn’t know or understand feeling … conception … volition … consciousness and doesn’t stop or become free of desire for it, it’s not possible to stop suffering.

「諸比丘，於色若知若明，若斷若離欲，則能斷苦。 “Monks, if someone knows and understands form and stops and becomes free of desire for it, it’s possible to stop suffering. t99.2.1b2 如是，受⋯想⋯行⋯識若知若明，若斷若離欲，則能堪任斷苦。」 Thus, if someone knows and understands feeling … conception … volition … consciousness and stops and becomes free of desire for it, it’s possible to end suffering.”

時，諸比丘聞佛所說歡喜，奉行。 When the monks heard what the Buddha taught, they rejoiced and approved. t99.2.1b5
