（四） 無知 7 (4). Not Knowing (2)

如是我聞： Thus I have heard: t99.2.1b6 一時，佛住舍衛國，祇樹，給孤獨園。 One time, the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in Jeta’s Grove of Śrāvastī.

爾時，世尊告諸比丘： It was then that the Bhagavān addressed the monks, t99.2.1b6 「於色不知不明，不斷不離欲，心不解脫者，則不能越生、老、病、死怖。 “When one doesn’t know or understand form and don’t stop or become free of desire for it, their mind isn’t liberated, and then they can’t transcend the fear of birth, old age, illness, and death. 欲 → 欲貪 (yinshun) 如是，受⋯想⋯行⋯識不知不明，不斷不離欲貪，心不解脫者，則不能越生、老、病、死怖。 Thus, when one doesn’t know or understand feeling … conception … volition … consciousness and doesn’t stop or become free of desire for it, their mind isn’t liberated, and then they can’t transcend the fear of birth, old age, illness, and death.

「比丘，於色若知若明，若斷若離欲，則能越生、老、病、死怖。諸比丘！若知若明，若離欲貪，心解脫者，則能越生、老、病、死怖。 “Monks, if someone knows and understands form and stops and becomes free of desire for it, their mind is liberated, and then they can transcend the fear of birth, old age, illness, and death. t99.2.1b7 若離欲，則能越生、老、病、死怖。諸比丘！若知若明， → () (yinshun) 如是，受⋯想⋯行⋯識若知若明，若斷若離欲貪，心解脫者，則能越生、老、病、死怖。」 Thus, if someone knows and understands feeling … perception … volition … consciousness and stops and becomes free of desire for it, their mind is liberated, and then they can transcend the fear of birth, old age, illness, and death.”

時，諸比丘聞佛所說歡喜，奉行。 When the monks heard what the Buddha taught, they rejoiced and approved. t99.2.1b10
