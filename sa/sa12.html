• 雜阿含經 12 The Related Discourses

（一二） 説因縁 18 (12). Cause and Condition (2)

如是我聞： Thus I have heard: t99.2.2b4 一時，佛住舍衛國，祇樹，給孤獨園。 One time, the Buddha was staying at Anāthapiṇḍada’s Park in Jeta’s Grove of Śrāvastī.

爾時，世尊告諸比丘： It was then that the Bhagavān addressed the monks, t99.2.2b5 「色無常。 “Form is impermanent. 若因若緣生諸色者，彼亦無常。 Whatever causes or conditions give rise to forms, they are also impermanent. 無常因無常緣所生諸色，云何有常？ How could forms that arise from impermanent causes and conditions be permanent? 受⋯想⋯行⋯識無常。 Feeling … conception … volition … consciousness is impermanent. 若因若緣生諸識者，彼亦無常。 Whatever causes or conditions give rise to consciousnesses, they are also impermanent. 無常因無常緣所生諸識，云何有常？ How could consciousnesses that arise from impermanent causes and conditions be permanent?

「如是，比丘，色無常。 “Thus, monks, form is impermanent, and feeling … conception … volition … consciousness is impermanent. t99.2.2b9 受⋯想⋯行⋯識無常。 What’s impermanent is painful, what’s painful is not self, and what’s not self doesn’t belong to self. 無常者則是苦，苦者則非我，非我者則非我所。 Such observation is called true and correct observation.

聖弟子如是觀者於色解脫，於受⋯想⋯行⋯識解脫。 “Noble disciples who thus observe are liberated from form and liberated from feeling … conception … volition … consciousness. t99.2.2b12 我說是等為解脫生，老，病，死，憂，悲，苦，惱。」 I say that this is equal to being liberated from birth, old age, illness, death, grief, sorrow, pain, and trouble.”

時，諸比丘聞佛所說，歡喜奉行。 When the monks heard what the Buddha taught, they rejoiced and approved. t99.2.2b14
